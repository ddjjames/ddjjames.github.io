Project Atlantis

3D Single-Player Adventure

Dive into the depths of adventure with this mesmerizing 3D puzzle game. As an experienced diver, you’ve been dispatched by a Private Company (Pacifica) to explore the mystifying ruins of an ancient underwater civilization. Funded by Pacifica you come equipped with the latest technology a digital codex and aqua vac your specimen collection tool, You set out to explore the secrets lurking beneath the waves but you soon learn that the secrets the depths hold are more profound than you could have ever anticipated. Collaborating seamlessly with other disciplines, I crafted innovative tools and streamlined workflows. I also delved into gameplay programming, in order to elevate the player experience. Brace yourself for a journey where mystery meets mastery, and the ocean conceals more than it reveals.

Tools

In this project, I tackled a major challenge that the artist on the team faced: painting multiple materials onto a landscape efficiently in Unreal. I created a custom landscape material that could accept multiple material inputs and alpha blend them. This enabled the artist to quickly iterate and create stunning environments with different textures and colors. The tool I developed was user-friendly and efficient, as it integrated with Unreal's built-in landscape paint tool and enabled quick switching between predefined materials. This tool showcases my skills in creating tools and workflows that enhance the artistic vision and productivity of the team.

Visual Effects

In this project, I worked closely with the team's visual effects artist to create stunning underwater effects. I acted as their Unreal expert and helped them with the technical aspects of their VFX.

I also improved the player feedback and immersion by using Runtime Virtual Textures, a feature that allows for dynamic texel data generation onto landscapes. Once this data was fed into a landscape material it would allow me to distort within the parameters of the provided texture. Custom textures could be used to define the look and size of the trail left by the player when swimming over the sea floor or suctioning with the aqua vac.
